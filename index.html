Overview

What DreamZero-SO101 is, and why it matters

01 — What it is

A joint video + action predictor

A 14B Wan2.1-I2V backbone + a 108M LoRA adapter. At each control step the model takes a single camera frame, a 6-DOF joint state, and a language instruction — and emits both a 0.3 s imagined video rollout and a 0.8 s (24-step) action chunk via flow matching, in one forward pass.

02 — What it does

Two deployment modes

In policy mode the model returns an action chunk at every real-robot step — 1.6–2.3° RMSE at cruise on unseen data, 11.9° mean on the full zero-shot test. In DreamGen mode it autoregressively imagines 60+ chunks of video + action from a single initial frame, executing the task inside its own imagination.

03 — Why it matters

An open entry point for SO-101

There is no open-sourced world-action model for the SO-101 arm yet. DreamZero-SO101 is the first — trained on community data, released under Apache 2.0, with a 217 MB LoRA that plugs into an unmodified Wan2.1-I2V backbone. We want this to become the foundation for future SO-101 policy research.
